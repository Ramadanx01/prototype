Materials Preparation Process

Step-by-step protocol for extracting and preparing the core components of the filter.

Overall process for Chitosan extraction.

Part 1: Chitosan Extraction

1. Deproteinization (Alkaline Treatment)

Boil washed shrimp shells in a 5-9% NaOH solution at 60-90°C for 2 hours. Wash until the pH is neutral.

2. Demineralization

Soak the shells in a 1-2% HCl solution at room temperature for 2 hours to remove minerals like calcium carbonate. Wash thoroughly.

3. Deacetylation (Chitin to Chitosan)

Heat the resulting chitin in a 40-60% NaOH solution at 90-100°C for 2 hours. This converts chitin into chitosan. Wash and dry the final product.

Yield: Approx. 15-25% of the initial shell weight.

Overall process for Nano-cellulose extraction.

Part 2: Nano-cellulose Extraction

1. Pre-treatment

Wash, dry, and grind rice straw into small ~5mm pieces.

2. Lignin & Hemicellulose Removal

Boil the straw in a 4-10% NaOH solution at 80-90°C for 2 hours to obtain purer cellulose. Wash and dry.

3. Bleaching

For final lignin removal, boil the cellulose in a 3-5% NaClO or H₂O₂ solution for 2 hours. This whitens the cellulose.

4. Acid Hydrolysis & Sonication

Convert cellulose to nano-cellulose by acidifying with H₂SO₄ for 60 minutes with manual stirring. Follow with mechanical treatment using an ultrasonicator to achieve nano-scale particles.

Detection Mechanism & Dyes

Using specific dyes, the system provides a rapid visual or sensor-based indication of contaminants.

Dyes for Contaminant Detection

Bromothymol Blue (BTB)

Purpose: pH Indicator

Detects changes in water acidity or alkalinity, which can indicate chemical pollution.

Yellow: pH < 6.0

Green: pH ≈ 7.0

Blue: pH > 7.6

Preparation: 2g in 100ml of 1% Acetic Acid solution.

Bromothymol Blue showing different pH colors.

Dithizone

Purpose: Heavy Metal Detection

Forms colored complexes with heavy metal ions (e.g., lead, mercury), causing a visible color change.

Preparation: 0.03g in 100ml Ethanol solution (Alcoholic Solution).

Dithizone reacting with heavy metals.

Fluorescein Diacetate (FDA)

Purpose: Microbial Activity Detection

A non-fluorescent compound that becomes fluorescent (green glow) when processed by enzymes in living microorganisms (bacteria).

Preparation: 0.01g in 0.2ml NH₄OH and 100ml water.

Fluorescence under UV light indicating microbial activity.

Data Analysis Workflow

Visualizing the dye reaction on the filter membrane.

1. Contact & Reaction

Contaminated water flows through the filter membrane, coming into contact with the embedded dyes, initiating a color change or fluorescence.

Optical sensor setup for real-time analysis.

2. Optical Sensing

An optical sensor (e.g., a spectrophotometer or color sensor) measures the change in color or fluorescence intensity, providing quantitative data.

Microcontroller board processing sensor data.

3. Digital Conversion

A microcontroller (like an Arduino or ESP32) converts the analog sensor reading into a digital value, preparing it for data processing.

Real-time water quality data displayed on a mobile device.

4. Data Display

The digital data is sent to a connected device (laptop or smartphone) for real-time display and analysis, providing an immediate assessment of water quality.

Prototype Results & Future Work

Key findings from prototype testing and future projections.

High Efficiency

We detected the pollution in water in a short period of time (after one minute from entering the water) with high efficiency.

Enhanced Surface Area

Converting cellulose to nanocellulose increased the surface area by 10–20 times, enhancing the layer's ability to bind dyes.

Stable Gel Formation

Mixing 60% chitosan with 40% nanocellulose produced a stable, viscous gel that could be easily molded onto a plate.

Bromothymol Blue

Exhibited subtle color changes with pH, with a rapid response (<1 minute).

Dithizone

Emitted distinct colors in the presence of heavy metal ions: red for lead, orange for copper, and pale green for zinc, allowing for both qualitative and quantitative detection.

Fluorescein Diacetate

Shown a green glow in the presence of live bacteria, demonstrating the dye's effectiveness in detecting microbes.

Future Work

  • Integration of AI/ML algorithms for more accurate pollutant classification and concentration estimation.
  • Scaling up membrane production using advanced techniques like electrospinning for wider availability.
